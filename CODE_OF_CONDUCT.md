# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in the
NutEV/NutMEV community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of
age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics,
gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic
status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and
orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming,
diverse, inclusive, and healthy community — and, because this is a scientific
project, to uphold research integrity: honesty, transparency, traceability of
claims, and respect for human oversight of any automated output.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:

- Demonstrating empathy and kindness toward other people.
- Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences.
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback.
- Accepting responsibility, apologizing to those affected by our mistakes, and
  learning from the experience.
- Focusing on what is best for the community and for scientific rigor.

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

- The use of sexualized language or imagery, and sexual attention or advances.
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks.
- Public or private harassment.
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission.
- Fabricating, falsifying, or misrepresenting evidence, or presenting automated
  output as a verified clinical recommendation.
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a
  professional or scientific setting.

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Community maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing standards of
acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in
response to any behavior deemed inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces and when an individual
is officially representing the community in public spaces.

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be
reported to the maintainers via a **private security/conduct report** (see
`SECURITY.md` for private contact, or open a confidential channel with the
maintainers). All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and
fairly. Maintainers are obligated to respect the privacy and security of the
reporter of any incident.
